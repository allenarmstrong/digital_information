Finding and Replacing

Oh no! You just realized you repeatedly referred to Edgar Allan Poe as Edgar Allen Poe like a hundred times in your English essay. That would normally take forever to fix, but the Find and Replace features can correct every instance of this error in a few seconds.

Find and Replace

Find and Replace

Follow these directions to find and replace text in your document:

  1. You have two options to find and replace text.
    1. Highlight the word you want to find and select "Find and Replace" from the Home Ribbon. The word will appear in the top box.
    2. Type the word you want to find in the top box.
  2. Type the word you want to use as a replacement in the "Replace with…" box.
  3. You can either replace individual instances or replace all instances of the word.
    1. Select the individual box in which the word appears and select "Replace."
    2. Select "Replace All" to replace all instances.

Look at the following example. Notice the writer decided to replace the last instance of the word "access" with the word "connection." By selecting the final box and then the "Replace" button, "access" is automatically replaced with "connection" in the last line.

Find Only

If you only need to see where a word, phrase, or punctuation mark is used in your document (like a word from your English vocabulary list), use the "Find" feature.

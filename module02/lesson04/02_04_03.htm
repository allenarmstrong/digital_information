Layout Matters

It's hard to imagine, but margins, orientation, and size can make a big difference. Look at the two pictures below. Notice how the different page layouts impact the significance of the snorkeler's experience. 

When it comes to creating a Word document, make sure you choose the page layout that will support your purpose and communicate your message clearly. Explore each tab below to learn more about the features within Page Layout.

Margins

Margins

Depending on your purpose, you may need to adjust the margins of your document.

To adjust the margins, do the following:

  1. Select Margins.
  2. Make a selection:
    1. Choose a preset Margin setting.
    2. If you need specific margins, select Custom Margins and enter the desired margin dimensions. Select "OK" when finished.
Orientation

Orientation

Portrait Orientation

Think about reading a text message. If you hold your cell phone vertically (as most people do), the message will be displayed taller rather than wider. We call this Portrait Orientation.

Notice how these portraits are tall and narrow:

Portrait is the default setting for Word documents. It is an effective orientation for the following:

  • articles
  • business letters
  • essays
  • memos
  • stories

Landscape Orientation

If you wanted to take picture of the beach, you wouldn't narrow in on just one grain of sand. Instead, you would opt for a wide view to capture all the different elements of the scenery. We call this Landscape Orientation.

Notice how these images show a wide view of the scene:

Landscape orientation is best suited for the following:

  • cards
  • charts
  • diagrams
  • invitations
  • signs
Size

Size

A word to the wise: One size does not always fit all. Certain Word documents require a different page size. Here are some examples:

  • cards
  • envelopes
  • legal documents
  • letters
  • postcards
  • recipe cards

Word documents default to letter size, but you can go to Size and select different page sizes depending on your purpose.

Dress Up Your Word Docs

There is no denying the ease and practicality of jeans and a t-shirt. The same is true for a Word document that uses standard black, Times New Roman, 12 point font text on a white background. But, you wouldn't wear jeans and a t-shirt to the prom. You would buy some bling, put on a tux, get your hair done. This same concept can be applied when creating a Word document. Compare and contrast the images below. Notice how inserting images, shapes, and clipart take a plain Word document from drab to fab:



Explore each tab below to learn how to make your plain Word documents look fabulous.

Inserting Pictures from
Your Computer

Inserting Pictures from Your Computer

Accessories are optional. Their main function is to enhance the look of an outfit. The same can be said about using pictures in a Word document. They aren't necessarily required, but they do add style. To insert a picture saved on your computer, follow these steps: 


  1. Go to the Insert tab on the Ribbon.
  2. Select Picture .
  3. Select Choose File to browse for the picture on your computer.
  4. Select the picture you want to insert in your document.
  5. Select the Insert button on the pop-up window.

Once you follow these steps, the picture saved on your computer will be inserted into your document.

Searching and Inserting
Online Pictures

Searching and Inserting Online Pictures

Maybe you don't already have the perfect picture saved to your computer. Have no fear! You can search online pictures and clip art to find and insert the ideal image by following these steps.

  1. Go to the Insert tab on the Ribbon.
  2. Select Online Pictures .
  3. Type a word or phrase that describes the picture you are looking for in the Search field and select the magnifying glass.
  4. Look through the list of results and select the image you would like to insert.
  5. Select the Insert button.

If you follow these steps, you can successfully search for and insert an online picture.

Formatting Pictures
 

Formatting Pictures

Just inserting a picture onto the page doesn't automatically make your document look good. Like choosing the right sunglasses, you have to try on several pairs to find a frame and style that complements your face. You will do the same thing when you insert pictures. Select your image and use the Format picture tool to add a cool frame around the pictue and adjust the size, so your images enhance your document.

Alternative Text
 

Alternative Text

It is important to know that some users won't be able to see your text and visuals. They’ll hear it! Whether due to technological limitations or visual impairment, many people require a text alternative to visual information. To add a written description of your image, select the image on your document then select the Alt-Text option from the Format tab. Type an image description in the blank field. Select "OK" when finished.

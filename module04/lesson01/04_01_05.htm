Ice Your Spreadsheet

In an Excel spreadsheet, your data is the most important element. However, the way you format your information can enhance your content—like icing on cake. You can modify the following elements in Excel Online, which will help clarify, emphasize, and differentiate your data:

  • column width and row height
  • font style and color
  • number formatting
  • text alignment

Hide and Seek


You have the ability to hide certain columns and rows on your spreadsheet, but it varies depending on the device you use.

  1. On both a computer and tablet, select the column letter or row number you would like to hide.
  2. On a computer, right-click to display the menu. On a tablet, select an open circle to display the menu.
  3. Select “Hide Columns” or “Hide Rows” from the menu.

There are several reasons why you would hide a column or row:

  • You may not need the data in the column or row now, but you may later.
  • It may be easier to analyze your data with certain columns or rows hidden.
  • You do not want a collaborator to see certain data.

If you would like to unhide columns or rows previously hidden, follow these directions based on your device:

  1. On both a computer and tablet, select the columns before and after or the rows above and below the one you want to unhide.
  2. On a computer, right-click to display the menu. On a tablet, select an open circle to display the menu.
  3. Select “Unhide Columns” or “Unhide Rows” from the menu.
To select multiple cells, columns, and/or rows on a computer, select and hold down the CTRL button while you select your desired fields.

Make a Better List

Data refers to information and can be anything from numbers and statistics to song titles and ice cream flavors. Take a grocery list, for example. The items on the list are a type of data. When you have a lot of data or the data frequently changes, you need a database.

A database is a collection of information stored electronically that can be easily modified, organized, and retrieved. Oracle and Microsoft Access are common database software, but an Excel spreadsheet can serve as a simple database, too.

Fun Fact

Why do we need databases to store information? Take a look at the numbers.

  • Alibris has over 100 million items (books, textbooks, music, and movies) in its online library.
  • eBay has 145 million active buyers.
  • iTunes has over 26 million songs in its catalog.
  • Whitepages.com has contact information for over 300 million people.

Though these figures change as new items are added or deleted, this gives you an idea of the huge amounts of information databases store.

Inquiring Minds Want to Know…

If you have ever downloaded a movie from Amazon, Hulu, iTunes, or Netflix, then you have used a database. You have most likely performed a query, too. With Netflix, it would take a long time to scroll through each movie. Therefore, you can perform a query to see a list of only certain types of movies—like comedies or thrillers. Databases allow you to search for information based on certain criteria instead of having to comb through all the data.

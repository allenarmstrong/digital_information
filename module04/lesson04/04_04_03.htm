Mail Merge

If you were going to make a homemade pizza for eight people, would you make it one slice at a time? Probably not. It's way easier and more efficient to make one whole pizza and then share it. Mail merge is like a pizza, except it's made of text. (Oh, and you don't eat it.) It is a feature of many word processors, like Microsoft Word. Mail merge allows you to create a form letter and personalize it for different recipients by using the information in your database.

Mail Merge Uses

Companies use mail merge with their customer databases to send personalized mass emails and advertisements. However, mail merge can be used in a variety of ways other than mass marketing.

Mail Merge in Action

Mrs. Sanchez often uses mail merge to communicate with her computer club members. Here are the steps she took to send a personalized certificate to each of the members:

  1. She created a database with the club members’ names and email addresses.
  1. She created a certificate template.
  1. She started a mail merge and used her club database to automatically fill each student's first name on the certificate, instead of having to customize each one individually.
  1. She used the same database to email each student's certificate, instead of having to send each one individually.

If Mrs. Sanchez had the student's home address in the database, she could also print out mailing labels or personalize the envelopes with the address.

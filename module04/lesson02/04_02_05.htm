On One Condition…

Guess what? You already know what a conditional formula is, you just don't know you know it.

How many times have you heard, "If you get a good report card, then you can get your phone back" or "If you clean your room, then you can go to the movies‚Äù? Those statements contain a condition, or requirement, that must be met in order to achieve a certain result. In Excel, conditional formulas are used to verify if data is true or false.

Example Formula:

Here is a conditional formula using the "IF" function, which is listed under the Commonly Used and Logical categories:
=IF(A1>6, TRUE)

This formula will verify whether the data in cell A1 is greater than the number 6.

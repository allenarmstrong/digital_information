Preventative Maintenance

Back to that car again—you know how your parents get the oil changed and tires rotated? That is to keep the car from breaking down. On a computer, you need to perform similar actions to prevent it from breaking. One extremely important task is to have anti-virus software loaded on the computer to protect it.

Virus Protection

What Is It?
Software or hardware that protects your computer by:
• searching for malicious files such as viruses, Trojan horses, and worms
• preventing spyware, adware, and other malware
• removing and destroying harmful files

Types
Anti-Virus
Protects against computer viruses that damage the computer or interfere with normal operations

Anti-Spyware
Protects against software that collects personal data from your computer without your knowledge

Firewall
Protects from threats that access your computer over the internet
 

Free vs. Paid
Free
Scaled-back version providing adequate protection against common invaders

Paid
Detects new versions of existing virus code and offers technical support

What Best Meets Your Needs?

Research reviews, ask an expert, or use a trial subscription to help
you choose the best option for you.

Stand-alone Product
• more advanced
• research and customize your security system
Security Suite
• easier to use
• wider range of protection
• tends to slow down computers
• not as thorough as stand-alone products
Built-in Firewalls
• found in Windows and Mac OSX
• must turn them on
Web Browser Security Settings
• limited protection provided
• must customize and disable features

Get the Most from Your Security

  • Turn on automatic update.
  • Renew subscriptions.
  • Always enable security software.
  • Restart your computer at least once a week.
  • Uninstall all anti-virus programs if you decide to change products.
  • Install software updates to fix vulnerabilities.
  • Use technical support with difficult problems.
  • Download from trusted sites.

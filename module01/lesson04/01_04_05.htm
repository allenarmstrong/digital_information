Workplace Communication

"Working from home" nowadays is a thing—and a really common one, at that. Many schools and companies today allow their students or employees to work remotely—meaning from home, a coffee shop, or, really, anywhere with Wi-Fi. Whether completing your online assignments or working from home, email and phone calls are great ways to stay connected, but those forms of communication are not enough.


Below are other tools used to keep people connected and productive:

Communication Method How Businesses Use Examples
Cloud Server Allows for file sharing between co-workers and offers a backup for data. Google Drive, OneDrive, Sharepoint, Dropbox, Box, iCloud
Instant Message (IM) Allows for instant communication on the computer or smartphone. Google Chat, Skype, FaceTime
Teleconference Allows a group of individuals to speak over the phone or computer to discuss issues. Skype (call feature), conferencecalling.com, freeconferencecall.com
Telepresence Allows individuals to attend meetings virtually with their image onscreen so all members feel like they are in the same room having the meeting. Skype, Google Hangouts, Blackboard

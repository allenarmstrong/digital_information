Organization Is Key

Organization on your computer is just as important as keeping your room clean. It is nice to have all your stuff in order, but it's not much fun putting it away. But less time searching for your phone, keys, or your favorite t-shirt means more time for fun, right?

The same concept is true with your computer files. Setting up a file structure means you will spend less time trying to find your final research paper for history class. You may think you have a method: save it to your desktop and it will be easy to find, right? Maybe at first, but once you have a hundred school files on your desktop, it is no longer easy to find anything. Now picture an alternative: you created folders that organize your schoolwork by grade and by subject with corresponding file names.

When you create folders, give them a title that makes sense and name files according to the content that is in them. This will make it easier for you to find the document when you are looking for it.

Naming Convention Tips:

  • use names that fit the content
  • make them alphanumeric (no symbols)
  • be consistent with capital letters
  • no spaces in the name (use _ in place of a space)

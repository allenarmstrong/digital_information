Getting the Gold

The job market is a lot like an Olympic event. Instead of a gold medal, though, you are competing against others for a paying job (which is as good as gold!). Before you ever meet a potential employer face-to-face, you will go through the pre-qualifying round by submitting your résumé and cover letter. These items make a first impression, so they need to showcase your skills, experience, education, and accomplishments in a winning way.


Rock Your Résumé

Think of your résumé as a first impression. On average, potential employers spend about 6.25 seconds reading your résumé. That means you have to make a BIG impact in a short amount of time. So how do you do that?

Look at the essential elements a résumé must have.

Get Noticed

In a field of daisies, be a sunflower. What does this mean in a job search? You have to make your résumé stand out from the rest in order to get noticed.

Here are seven strategies to catch your reader's attention and keep them reading:

  1. Make your name BIG and bold at the top. This should grab your reader's eye.
  2. Use keywords to emphasize skills and achievements.
  3. Make these action words.
  4. Stick to one or two font styles and make them easy to read. Struggling to decipher fancy fonts will only frustrate your reader.
  5. Use a larger font size for headings and a smaller font size for content. This is a great way to chunk information in a visually-organized manner.
  6. Use bullet points sparingly in any one section. Aim for no more than five to seven to avoid bullet overload.
  7. Add some color, carefully. A pop of color will make your résumé stand apart from the stack of black and white ones.
  8. Proofread, proofread, proofread! Spelling and punctuation really do make a difference.

Help Wanted

Do you want a career someday? If so, you will need two types of skills: hard skills and soft skills. Different occupations require different sets of hard and soft skills, and some believe your hard skills will get you the interview while your soft skills may actually get you the job. Let's look at two careers and their necessary skills.

Hard Skills

  • Understand anatomy and physiology
  • Know a massive number of illnesses
  • Interpret test results
  • Diagnose patients
  • Prescribe medication

Soft Skills

  • Compassion
  • Listening
  • Sensitivity
  • Caring
  • Trustworthiness

Hard Skills

  • HTML5
  • CSS
  • JavaScript
  • Color theory
  • Design knowledge

Soft Skills

  • Communication
  • Patience
  • Innovation
  • Creativity
  • Teamwork

Whether you want to become a carpenter, doctor, graphic designer, mechanic, or web developer, you can learn the hard skills through proper education and training. Soft skills, on the other hand, are not as easily learned because they are more like personality traits. Take every opportunity you can to strengthen your hard skills (take a class, read a book, do some research, etc.) and plump up your soft ones (be on time, listen to others, work in groups, etc.).

Fun Fact

Changes in society and technology are driving the need for new skills in the workplace. The question is: Do you have them? According to Top 10 Online Colleges, these will be the top 10 most important work skills in 2020:

  • Sense making: The ability to determine the deeper meaning or significance of what is being expressed
  • Social intelligence: The ability to connect with others in a deep and direct way; to sense and stimulate reactions and desired interactions
  • Novel and adaptive thinking: The ability to think of solutions and responses beyond what is traditional or expected
  • Cross cultural competency: The ability to operate in different cultural settings
  • Computational thinking: The ability to translate huge amounts of data into abstract concepts and to understand data-based reasoning
  • New Media Literacy: The ability to critically evaluate and develop content that uses new media forms and to use these media for persuasive communication
  • Transdisciplinary: The ability to understand concepts across multiple subjects
  • Design Mindset: The ability to represent and develop tasks and work processes for desired outcomes
  • Cognitive load management: The ability to distinguish and organize information for importance and to understand how to maximize logical functions
  • Virtual collaboration: The ability to work productively, drive engagement, and demonstrate presence as a member of a virtual team

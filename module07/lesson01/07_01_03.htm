Design Makes a Difference

PowerPoint comes with preformatted slide designs, which can be accessed in the Design tab. While there are many awesome designs, you need to select designs based on your purpose and audience. For example, if you are presenting a proposal to City Council members to add a stop sign at a busy intersection, a flowery design would not be professional or serious enough.

Roll over each slide to see what types of presentations are appropriate.

Design 1

This solid, straight forward design is appropriate for a serious presentation like a business proposal or academic report.

Design 2

The flowery design and picture of a little girl would be great for a student who is presenting her autobiography, or for a person making a tribute to a family member or friend.

Design 3

The basketball theme is appropriate for several things like a report on the history of basketball, a presentation to fundraise for the basketball team, or even a financial report for a sporting goods store. When using a theme (sports, nature, books, etc.), you should select based on the subject of your presentation.

Design 4

The geometric shapes are visually appealing yet neutral enough to be appropriate for business or academic presentations, or even a creative project.

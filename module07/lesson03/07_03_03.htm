Color, Contrast, and Clarity

Contrasting colors carefully create clarity. This sounds like a tongue-twister, but it's actually important PowerPoint advice. When customizing a PowerPoint presentation, you will notice that content appears clearer when there is a high degree of contrast between the text color and the background color, making it easier for your audience to read.

In addition, certain color combinations (like red text on a blue background) can even cause eye strain. Other combinations (like red text on a green background) can be almost unreadable to those who suffer with color blindness and can't tell the difference between green and red. To avoid mixing the wrong colors, don't mix warm and cool color groups.

Showing Progress

Back in elementary school, you may have participated in "Show and Tell." This is when you bring something from home—like your favorite stuffed animal, perhaps—and explain why it's special. Visual aids grab the audience's attention (which is why your classmates probably spent more time looking at your beloved teddy bear than listening to why you loved him so much). Therefore, you must select graphical and multimedia elements purposefully. Your visuals should clearly show, so you don't have to over tell.

Use the Insert tab to add graphical and multimedia elements to your PowerPoint presentation.

The Slides tab allows you to add a new slide and choose its layout.

The Tables tab allows you to select the number of columns and rows you need in your table.

Images allows you to add Pictures stored on your computer or Online Pictures from the web.

Illustrations allows you to add Shapes (basic shapes, lines, flowcharts, stars, etc.) or SmartArt (Venn diagrams, process lists, pyramids, etc.).

The Hyperlink tab links your text to a website, a picture, a page, an email address, etc.

The Text Box tab allows you to create a text box in which to type new text.

The Comments tab adds notes to your document.

The Symbol tab adds various symbols to your document.

The Online Video tab allows you to search YouTube and add an online video from its database.

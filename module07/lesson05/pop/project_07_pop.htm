07.05 Module Seven Project

Directions

Now it's time to apply what you've learned. Please complete each step in order and follow all directions carefully.


Step One: Questions to Get You Started

Use the following questions to start your personal reflection. Your answers will help you complete the requirements of your Module Seven Project.

  1. What are your hobbies and interests?
  2. What do you do for fun?
  3. What skills, sports, activities, etc. would you like to learn to do?
  4. What are your favorite books, clothing, food, movies, music, subjects, etc.?
  5. What colors represent your personality?
  6. Who are your role models? How do they influence you?
  7. What are your academic strengths? How did you acquire these?
  8. What are your personal strengths? How did you acquire these?
  9. What are your academic weaknesses? What caused this? How can you improve this area?
  10. What are your personal weaknesses? What caused this? How can you improve this area?
  11. How would others describe you? Do you agree? What would you like to change about this perception?
  12. What do you like about yourself?
  13. What is an area of your life you would like to change? How can you achieve this?
  14. Where do you see yourself in five years? Ten years? Fifty years?
  15. What would you like to do after graduation?

Step Two: Project Guidelines

As you begin your Module Seven Project, remember: Be creative and have fun! You have the skills to create a visually appealing and meaningful presentation, but you also have an opportunity to get to know yourself better and evaluate your life. So dig deep and express yourself.

You may want to print the following checklist to ensure your presentation includes all the necessary criteria. Your PowerPoint presentation must include the following elements:

  1. Theme
    • Theme reflects your personality and the overall mood and tone of the presentation.
    • Theme is applied to all slides.
  2. Slides
    • Presentation includes a total of 12 slides.
    • Four or more content layouts are used to organize content clearly and logically.
    • Title slide has the following:
      • Original presentation title
      • Your full name
    • Content slides have the following:
      • Table of contents
        • Lists the title of each slide and slide number
      • Family tree
        • List either your family tree through your grandparents or the people who live in your home, starting with the eldest
        • Use SmartArt
      • Interests (i.e. hobbies, sports, activities, etc.)
        • Use a numbered list with your favorite listed as number 1
      • Favorites (i.e. food, music, places, books, movies, etc.)
        • Use bullets to list
      • Your biggest influence and the impact on your life
      • Strengths and what you like about yourself
      • Weaknesses and how you plan to improve these areas
      • Biggest challenge(s) and how you have overcome
      • Goals for your future
        • Hyperlink to a college or vocational school you would like to attend, a business where you would like to work, a social cause you would like to fight for, etc.
      • Meaningful quote that showcases your personality
    • Conclusion slide with the following:
      • Note to your younger self with helpful advice from what you have learned
  3. Content
    • Content shows true reflection by using specific examples and details.
    • Content is communicated clearly and concisely, as if the presentation will be elaborated on verbally.
    • Content is not wordy.
  4. Graphics
    • Images
      • Four or more total images, which are relevant and enhance the slide content, are included.
      • One self-portrait (a selfie will work) is included.
    • Video
      • One video, which relates to and enhances the slide content, is used.
    • Shapes
      • Three different shapes are used to emphasize content.
  5. Font and Color
    • More than one font size and style is used
      • Font size is large enough to read clearly.
      • Font style is consistent with overall theme.
    • Color choices complement the overall theme.
    • Contrast between font color and background color makes text easy to read.
  6. Transitions and Animations (remember to not overuse)
    • At least two slides have transitions to emphasize content.
    • At least two slides have animations to emphasize content.
  7. Creativity
    • Content is presented in a visually appealing way.
    • Content is written in an engaging manner.
    • Content is conveyed in an original, fresh way. (Try a Google search for "Cool PowerPoint Examples" to see the countless ways to create innovative slides.)
  8. Mechanics
    • Proper spelling, grammar, and punctuation are used throughout presentation.

Step Three: Save

Save your PowerPoint presentation to your OneDrive using the file name format, "LastName_FirstName_M7L5." For example, if your name is Jane Smith, you would save the file as "Smith_Jane_M7L5."

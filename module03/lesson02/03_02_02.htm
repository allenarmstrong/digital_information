Workplace Essentials

Successful companies and employees know the value of four essential elements: customer service, business ethics, workplace policies, and human relations. In fact, these essentials are often what set apart the best from the rest.

Select each slide to see the workplace essentials.

Customer Service

There is a saying about customer service: It takes months to find a customer and only seconds to lose one. According to the Small Business Administration, "Small businesses which put heavy emphasis on customer service were more likely to survive and succeed than competitors who emphasized such advantages as lower prices or type of product." Providing quality customer service is a sure way to keep existing customers and entice new ones.

Business Ethics

A bad reputation can kill a career. For example, a wedding planner who leaks pictures to the press won’t get a call from red-carpet celebrities who value confidentiality. Likewise, a reporter guilty of plagiarism shouldn't expect many job offers; copyright violations are illegal. Successful businesses know the importance
of having ethical practices.

Rules and Regulations

There is an easy way to stay out of trouble: follow the rules. Certain workplace policies—like those against discrimination, sexual harassment, and smoking—are actually state and/or federal laws and must be followed. Other rules that deal with things like dress code, cell phone use, and tardiness are optional. Businesses choose policies and procedures that make the workplace more efficient and productive.

Human Relations

The most successful businesses know that people are the most important asset. This is why companies with superior human relations tend to top the charts. The process of training employees, addressing their needs, fostering a creative and caring culture, and resolving conflicts between different employees or between employees and management is crucial for a happy and productive workplace.

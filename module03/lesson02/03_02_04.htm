Let's Get Personal

The National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE) used 260 survey responses (many of them from big corporations like Chevron and IBM) to compile a list of the top 10 most-wanted skills in potential employees. And to obtain the first five on the list you only need two things: strong personal and interpersonal skills.

Top 10 Most-Wanted Skills in Potential Employees

  1. Ability to work in a team structure
  2. Ability to make decisions and solve problems
  3. Ability to communicate verbally with people inside and outside an organization
  4. Ability to plan, organize, and prioritize work
  5. Ability to obtain and process information
  6. Ability to analyze quantitative data
  7. Technical knowledge related to the job
  8. Proficiency with computer software programs
  9. Ability to create and/or edit written reports
  10. Ability to sell and influence others

Personal Skills

An employee with strong personal skills can be described as responsible, dependable, and punctual (on time). He or she has integrity, maintains a positive attitude, takes initiative, and demonstrates respect for self and others. His or her attire (clothing) is clean and appropriate for the job.

Interpersonal Skills

Having superior interpersonal skills is invaluable when it comes to the workplace. Interpersonal skills include the following:

Oral exchange of information; speaking
Exchange of information without speaking; i.e. body language or writing
Ability to pay attention to and interpret what other people are saying
The act of working with someone to produce or create something
Working with others to find a mutually agreeable outcome
Working with others to identify, define, and solve problems
Exploring and analyzing options to make sound decisions

How many of these skills do you already have? Keep in mind, personal and interpersonal skills help us in our everyday life as well as the workplace, and you don't need to take any special classes to improve them. The next time you have soccer practice, make it a goal to arrive five minutes early. Or when you're faced with a big decision, look at all the options, talk to others who have experience in the area, and analyze which option is best. You are surrounded by opportunities to practice these essential skills, so take advantage!

Houston, We Have a Problem

In life, you can follow this general rule: The question is not if there will be a problem but when.

Whether you're in school or the workforce, at some point you can expect to work in a group, which can be challenging. Since preventing problems is not always possible, knowing and using conflict resolution strategies is critical.

Here is a conflict resolution strategy that focuses on respecting people's differences and achieving a win-win solution.

  • Identify the problem – Listen carefully to all parties involved in order to pinpoint the real issue. Ask questions to get to the root of the problem and define it.
  • Agree upon the problem – In order to move forward, all parties must agree what the issue actually is. If the parties disagree, paraphrase responses until the problem is clarified.
  • Brainstorm possible solutions – Bring all parties together to come up with possible solutions. This will solve the problem faster, and all parties will feel like they had a role in the resolution.
  • Negotiate a solution – Use the ideas generated during the brainstorming session to come up with a solution that is acceptable to everyone involved.

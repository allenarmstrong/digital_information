Start Your (Search) Engine


How long do you think it would take to search by hand through hundreds of millions of random documents to find information on social media? Who cares! Search engines like Google, Bing, and Yahoo! have revolutionized research and inquiry. Now, instead of heading to a library, using a card catalog to find possible resources, and then reading through countless pages to get the needed information, search engines can be opened in a browser on computers, smartphones, tablets, and other devices with internet access. However, with the vast amount of web pages, sometimes simply typing a word into Google gets too many results or results that aren't quite right. Using better search tactics can improve your search results.

Here are some tips:

  • Focus on keywords, which are words used in a web search to find relevant web pages.
  • Use specific terms: for example, "red bicycle" instead of just bicycle.
  • Use quotation marks (" ") to search for exact phrases (i.e. "solar systems," "social media," "feta cheese").
  • Do not use unnecessary articles (like "the" or "a"), prepositions (like "to" or "from"), or pronouns (like "he" or "they"), which won't help the search. Stick to concrete nouns.
  • Use correct spelling and capitalization to optimize results.

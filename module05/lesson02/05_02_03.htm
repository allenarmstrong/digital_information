Your Wish is GUIs Command

In 2014, Americans spent an average of 159 minutes on the internet each day. So anything that can make surfing, researching, checking email, finding addresses, and visiting favorite websites faster and easier is a good thing. GUI lets you configure your browser any way you wish.

Here are the basics:


Home Page

Home Page

Depending on which browser you use (Internet Explorer, Chrome, Firefox, Safari, etc.), the directions for setting your home page vary slightly. However, all browsers have a "Settings" tab where you can locate the option to set your homepage. When you enter the web page URL address and save your selection, your browser will automatically open to your designated page.

Bookmarks

Bookmarks

Regular bookmarks mark a certain place in a book, but on the computer, a bookmark is used to store your favorite websites in one location. Most browsers have a bookmark icon (like a star) on the toolbar that you can select in order to bookmark a website. When you open your bookmarks, you can select a website to visit, delete a website you no longer want saved, change the default website name, and re-arrange your list alphabetically.

Preferences

Preferences

Would you like your browser to show the home button on the toolbar? Is there a certain location where you want downloads to go? What font style and size is easiest for you to read? These are just some examples of the different settings you can configure in order to make your browser more efficient and enjoyable. The "Settings" tab is where you go to manage your preferences.

Email

Email

To configure your email on a computer, smartphone, tablet, or other device (like a Kindle), you need your email service provider, your username, and your password; your device will verify your email settings. Occasionally though, you may have to do a manual setup. In this case, you need the following information to complete the configuration: the type of email server your email provider uses for incoming and outgoing email, and the incoming and outgoing server addresses. The good news is you don't need to understand the details of these types of servers, just what kind your email uses.

Security

Security

Browsers have security settings to protect your computer from unsafe websites and safeguard your privacy. The browser has default security settings, but you can also configure the browser's security to suit your needs. The "Settings" tab gives you the option to manage how websites monitor your online activity by deciding which cookies are stored, how websites are tracked, whether to block or allow pop-ups, and customize the security settings to reduce the risk of viruses, malware, or hackers.

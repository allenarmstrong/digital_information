Let's Network It Out!

With over three billion internet users worldwide (and growing each nanosecond), the ability to connect with others around the globe—whether for business or personal purposes—is astounding. To understand how this connection is actually possible, you must explore the various types of networks a computer can use to access the internet.

These are the most common network types:

Campus Area Network: A network consisting of multiple LANs but smaller than a WAN; typically seen in universities and office buildings
Local Area Network: A computer network limited to a small area such as an office building, university, or a home
Metropolitan Area Network: A network covering an area (a city) that is larger than a LAN but smaller than a WAN; typically owned and managed by a single entity
System Area Network: A network that provides access to data storage, so that storage devices can communicate with computers and other devices
System Area Network: A high performing network that connects computer clusters
Wide Area Network: A network that covers a broad area (like across metropolitan, regional, or national boundaries) using private or public networks
Wireless Local Area Network: A LAN that is used on wireless technology

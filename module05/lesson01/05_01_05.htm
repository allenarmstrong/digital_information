The Other Nets

In addition to the internet, there are two other types of nets used frequently in business settings: intranet and extranet. (Here is a prime example of when knowledge of root words comes in handy, by the way.)

Intranet

An organization, like a school or company, has an intranet so staff can access and share internal information quickly and safely. Intranets streamline communication, quickly circulate news and information, and allow staff to work from remote locations. Since many organizations keep copyrighted information on intranets, security is important. To access an intranet, authorized users log into the organization's network with a username and password.

Extranet

When an organization hires outside vendors to provide additional services, the vendors are given limited access to the organization's intranet. This practice saves time for everyone and reduces email usage. Most times, vendors are not given access to an organization's entire intranet in order to protect private information and data. This is one way for an organization to maintain regulatory control over who accesses its intranet.

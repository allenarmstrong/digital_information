Inside the Internet

Yes. You know what the internet is. It's what you connect to in order to access Instagram, use Google, and watch YouTube videos. But have you ever wondered what the internet looks like on the inside?

If you could take an x-ray of the internet, you would see it is comprised of the following:

  • The pages we view: GUI (graphic user interface), which contain a header, title, body, links, and screen resolution.
  • The coding and formatting behind the pages we view: http, https, CSS, JavaScript, DHTML, and HTML.
  • The different web browsers with cross-platform compatibility available for most operating systems: Microsoft Internet Explorer, Apple Safari, Google Chrome, and Mozilla Firefox.

That's the basic skeleton, though there are millions of veins, tendons, and ligaments (also known as networks) running throughout the internet too.

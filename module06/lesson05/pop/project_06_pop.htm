06.05 Module Six Project

Directions

You will be designing a web page that will consist of a heading title, an image, a link, an ordered list, a footer (your name and email link), text colors, and page formatting. Complete each step in order and read the directions carefully.


Step One: Choose Your Topic

Select one of the options below. Your selection will be the focus of your web page's content.

  • Personal Blog – Love to cook? Obsessed with sports? Trendy Fashionista? Choose an interest and tell your audience about it from your point of view.
    • The best things about (fill in subject) are:
  • Social Cause – Choose a cause that is important to you (animal rights, bullying, texting and driving, etc.) and persuade others to help.
    • Here are three ways you can help (insert your social cause goal):
  • Business Advertisement – Do you want to advertise your dog-walking business or babysitting services? Create a web page to advertise your business.
    • These are three services (add business name) provides:

Step Two: Storyboard

  1. Use the Storyboard Template.
  2. Complete each section of the storyboard in order to plan the content, layout, and design of your web page.
    Leave the Webpage URL section blank, since you will not actually have a URL for this project.
  3. Create a New Folder on your computer and name it "M6L5 Project."
  4. Save your chosen image(s) as a Jpeg (.jpg) file to your M6L5 Project folder.
  5. Save your work as "LastName_FirstName_M6L5_Storyboard" to your M6L5 Project folder. For example, if your name is Joe Smith, your file would be saved as "Smith_Joe_M6L5_Storyboard.”

Step Three: Create Your Web Page

Follow these directions to create a blank text editor and add your HTML code:

  1. Sign in to your Office Online account.
  2. Open your OneDrive.
  3. Select "New.”
  4. Select "Plain Text."
  5. Assign the file name as "Last Name_First Name_M6L5_Page." For example, if your name is Joe Smith, your file would be saved as "Smith_Joe_M6L5_Page."
    Your document will automatically be saved as a text (.txt) file.
  6. Enter your HTML code for the items listed on your storyboard. (Use the HTML Cheat Sheet for frequently used tags.) Your web page must include the following attributes for optimal design:
    • Alignment – Use center, right, and left alignment for text to create an organized and visually appealing layout.
    • Color – Use font colors and a background color based on color theory principles to create a visually appealing page design.
    • Emphasis – Use italics and bolding to effectively emphasize important text.
  7. Rename your file with the ending .html.
  8. Download your saved file to your M6L5 Project folder.

Step Four: Zip Your Files

Get ready to zip your work. Since your completed HTML code is telling the computer to look in the M6L5 folder to find your image and document link(s), if you only attach the .html file for grading, your web page will not allow your instructor to properly view your image or document link. This could result in a lower grade.

  1. Select your M6L5 Project folder.
  2. Right-click on the M6L5 Project folder and select Send To > Compress (zipped) folder.
    When you do this your files inside this folder will be converted into a compressed file, which looks like a folder with a zipper on it.
  3. Submit your zipped folder to the 06.05 Module Six Project in your assessments area.

Build Your Gallery

Walt Disney, the founder of the Walt Disney Company, said, "Of all of our inventions for mass communication, pictures still speak the most universally understood language." Images are an asset to web pages, so it's important to know how to obtain them. Here are various ways to access and digitize pictures:

  • Image hosting sites like Flickr, Tinypic, and Photobucket are free image hosting sites. You can search image hosting sites by subject to find photos for your web page.
  • Stock photo sites, like ThinkStock and iStock, are great for finding images. They cover a wide array of topics and are royalty-free. However, the downside is most stock photo sites require a membership fee.
  • Clip art is typically included as part of a software program or available online through clip art websites. Clip art represents many different topics.
  • Digital cameras and camera phones allow users to take their own pictures and copy the prints to a computer via a scanner or upload the digital image to the computer via a Universal Serial Bus (USB) connection. Many printers have built in scanners that allow users to place a photo face down on the scanner window and transmit the image to the connected computer. Use a USB to connect your camera or phone to the computer.
Some graphics have usage restrictions on them. Be aware of the permissions for a photo to protect yourself from violating any copyright laws.

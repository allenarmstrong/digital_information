If you are going to build a house, you need a blueprint. And if you're going to create a web page, you need a wireframe and a storyboard. A wireframe is a mock-up of your web page that sketches out where text and images will be placed, so you get a visual image of the page's layout. Once you have the wireframe, begin the storyboard which is essentially an outline of your web page's content and design. These planning tools show you the big picture and allow you to make revisions if necessary. Maybe your color scheme doesn't complement your graphics or your text is too long. It's easier and more efficient to make changes to your wireframe and storyboard than your actual web page.

Here are five tips for successful wireframing and storyboarding:

  1. Establish the purpose of your web page. Why are you creating the web page? What do you hope to achieve? What is your message? Who will your audience be?
  2. Gather information about your audience. Who are they? What do they like? What are the trends with this audience? How can you keep them interested?
  3. Find design inspiration! Go to your favorite web pages and see how color, fonts, and images are used. Look at the placement of headings and text, too. What do you like about these web pages, and what do you think could be improved?
  4. Add the visual appeal. Use what you have learned about your audience and what you have seen on popular web pages to choose a color scheme, font style, and images that will enhance your overall design.
  5. Reflect and revise. After your storyboard is complete, take an objective look at your sketch and determine areas that could be enhanced. Your colors, images, and text should all work together to create a cohesive, purposeful, and eye-catching web page that users will want to return to.

06.01 Building Blocks for Web Design

Directions

You are about to create a basic web page with a title, header, text, numbered list, text and background color, text formatting, text alignment, and an image. Read the directions carefully and follow the steps in order.


Step One: Create a Web Page

  1. Sign in to your Office Online account.
  2. Open your OneDrive.
  3. Create a New Folder on your computer and name it "M6L1 Assignment."
  4. Select "New" in OneDrive.
  5. Select Plain Text.
  6. Assign the following file name "LastName_FirstName_M6L1_Page." For example, if your name is John Smith, your filed will be saved as "Smith_John_M6L1_Page."
    Your document will automatically be saved as a text (.txt) file.
  7. Enter the following HTML code. Note: You must fill in the highlighted areas with your own content. 
    <!DOCTYPE html> 
<html> 
<head>
<title>Your Last Name_First Name_M6L1_Page</title> 
</head> 
<body style="background-color:Insert Color;">
<h1>Your Name</h1> 
<p>A sentence that describes you. Three of my favorite things are:</p> 
<ol><li><strong>First Favorite</strong></li> 
<li><em>Second Favorite</em></li> 
<li style="color:Insert Color;">Third Favorite</li></ol> 
<center><img src="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/236x/11/10/4b/11104b813f4698b2458115184793e697.jpg"></center>
</body> 
</html>
  8. Rename your file with the ending .html and save it to your "M6L1 Assignment" folder.
  9. Download your file to view it in your browser as a web page.

Step Two: Save and Reflect

  1. Save the Building Blocks for Web Design Reflection document as "LastName_FirstName_M6L1_Reflection." For example, if your name is John Smith, save your file as "Smith_John_M6L1_Reflection."
  2. Complete the reflection.
  3. Upload your finished document to your "M6L1 Assignment" in your OneDrive.

Step Three: Zip and Submit

Get ready to zip your work. To ensure your teacher can view items saved to your OneDrive, you need to zip your files. Follow these directions:

  1. Open your OneDrive.
  2. Select your "M6L1 Assignment" folder.
  3. Right-click on the "M6L1 Assignment" folder and select Send To > Compress (zipped) folder.
  4. When you do this, your files inside this folder will be put into a compressed file, which looks like a folder with a zipper on it.
  5. Submit your zipped folder to the 06.01 Building Blocks for Web Design in your assessments area.

Taking Stock of Images


As John learned previously, graphics are important to a website because they improve a website's appearance and will keep a reader on a site longer. He continues to read articles and comes upon one called "All About Graphics" about web graphics. He learns there are numerous avenues from which to obtain graphics. One of the ways to get graphics is via a scanner. A scanner is a device that is capable of copying a graphic, document, or other object and then converting the scan to a digital image. Many printers have scanners built in to them. He can simply place a photo on to the scanner window face down, and the scanner will copy and transmit it to his connected computer. Another route is via a digital camera. With a digital camera, he can photograph whatever he wants to and upload it to his computer, normally via a Universal Serial Bus (USB) connection. Images can also be stored on CD-ROMs and then uploaded to a computer.
